Russell's three puzzles and his solutions.

Puzzle 1: George IV and identity

If Scott is the author of Waverley, substituting gives: George IV wished to know whether Scott was Scott. That is absurd.

Puzzle 2: Excluded middle

Either the King of France is bald or not bald - but we find him in neither list. Russell: the denial has a secondary reading that is true.

Puzzle 3: The difference

When A and B differ there is a difference; when they do not, there is no such entity. How can a non-entity be the subject?
